The Bernese Mountain Dog

Overview

The Bernese Mountain is a robust and sturdy Swiss Mountain Dog. These dogs are a perfect exapmle of a breed designed to withstand rough environments and harsh cold climates. These dogs developed in the farmlands of Switzerland where they herded cattles and became loyal companions. These canines are acute and very intelligent. Their calm and friendly nature is perfect for active outdoors living.

Physique

This dog is very muscular and massive. It can weigh up to 120 pounds and reach a height of 28 inches. Its body is very muscular and tough which allow it to withstand any environment. It has a tricolor coat consisting of shades of black, white, and rust. It's chest is white while its legs and cheeks are highlighted by the rust color. The rust pigment on its face makes this breed appear as if it has eyebrows. It's coat is very thick and long allowing it to live in very cold climates. It is a very tolerant dog that is useful for work.

Personality

Calm and watchful, the Bernese is loyal and friendly. Also intelligent, this breed of dog is easy to train. This dog is perfect for families because of their low aggresiveness. Their cautios attitudes make them wary of strangers, so introduction to other people and environments is important. Bernese puppies are very active and playful. They tend to chew and nip at objects during this phase of their lives. With training and socialization, these dogs will be a loyal companion.

Health

This dog has a lifespan of 6 to 9 years. The Bernese is a large dog that requires a large space to keep a healthy weight. Regular exercise is recommended to ensure this breed keeps a healthy weight. The Bernese is prone to diseases such as: Willebrand's Disease (vWD), hypomyelination, allergies, hypothyroidism, hepatocerebellar degeneration and progressive retinal atrophy (PRA). The minor diseases that the dog is likely to suffer from are cataract, sub-aortic stenosis (SAS). Be sure to schedule visits to a vet to make sure your dog lives a healthy and happy life.

Nutrition

The Bernese Mountain dog has to eat healthy fresh foods in order to keep its athletic and muscular physique. Raw or cooked meat, fish, and eggs are important to ensure your dog gets protein. This dog also needs fruits such as bananas,apples, and pears to have a healthy amount of enzymes in his or her system. Vegetables such as carrots, broccoli, and squash will help keep you dog healthy
